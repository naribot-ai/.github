# NariBot – Paise Ki Saathi 🚺💰

**NariBot** is an AI-powered livelihood navigation system designed to support women across India’s grassroots economy.

Built with a **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) mindset**, NariBot aims to make livelihood guidance, income pathways, and opportunity discovery accessible through simple phone-based interfaces.

The project is being built in public as part of a broader effort to design **inclusive AI infrastructure for the last mile.**

**NariBot** is a **real-world last-mile deployment use case for AI infrastructure**, built **by a woman founder from India, for women in Bharat and the world**💃🏽🔱

---

## Why NariBot Exists

India has one of the world’s largest networks of women-led grassroots economic groups.

Yet millions of women still struggle to access:

• livelihood guidance  
• local work opportunities  
• relevant government schemes  
• market and income intelligence  
• decision support for micro-enterprises  

Most digital tools assume:

• smartphones  
• stable internet  
• high digital literacy  

NariBot starts from a different premise:

**Technology must adapt to the phone she already has.**

---

## What NariBot Does

NariBot acts as a **livelihood navigation assistant** that helps women:

• discover local work opportunities  
• explore income pathways  
• understand government schemes  
• ask livelihood questions in their own language  
• access practical guidance for small businesses  

The system is designed for **short, clear, real-world responses** suited for everyday decision-making.

---

## Accessibility First

NariBot prioritizes accessibility for women who may not regularly use smartphones or internet apps.

The platform is designed to work through **low-bandwidth interfaces**, including:

• **IVR (Voice interaction)**  
• **USSD (dial-based navigation)**  
• **Feature-phone-friendly chat systems**  

The goal is simple:

**Information and opportunity should be reachable from any phone.**

---

## Architecture Philosophy

NariBot is designed as a **modular ecosystem** so that different layers of the system can evolve independently while remaining interoperable.

Key principles include:

• Vernacular-first interaction  
• Low-bandwidth design  
• Scalable AI infrastructure  
• Modular orchestration layers  
• Public infrastructure alignment  

---

## Design Principles

NariBot is guided by a few simple principles while building technology for the last mile:

• **Phone before App**  
Solutions must work on the devices people already use.

• **Vernacular First**  
Language should never be a barrier to opportunity.

• **Low Bandwidth by Default**  
Systems should function even with limited connectivity.

• **Assistive AI, Not Replacing Humans**  
Technology should support local ecosystems and institutions.

• **Short Answers, Real Decisions**  
Information must be practical and usable in everyday life.

• **Built for Population Scale**  
Infrastructure must work for millions, not just early adopters.
